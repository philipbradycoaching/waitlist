The gap

We track everything.
We understand almost nothing.

Wearables collect thousands of data points a day. But nobody has built the layer between that data and your actual behaviour — the moments when your body is already telling you something your thinking mind hasn't caught up with yet.

Biometrics without context

Your HRV drops before a difficult conversation. Your heart rate spikes when you think about a decision. The data is there — but nothing translates it into action.

Reactivity in the moment

Most poor decisions and emotional responses happen when the nervous system is dysregulated — not because of bad judgment, but because the body is in protection mode.

Resilience that doesn't stick

Meditation apps build habits. Therapy builds insight. But neither gives you a live signal in the moment when your capacity to feel and respond is actually being tested.

What we're building

Two tools.
One nervous system.

Built together, used separately. Each one does a different job — but they're grounded in the same idea: your body is already tracking what matters.

For personal development

Regulate

Build your capacity to feel difficult emotions without being overwhelmed. Identify what's underneath patterns like procrastination, avoidance, or anger — then work through it using your body's own signals.

  • Two modes: regulate through a challenge or sit with curiosity about what's present
  • Body scan and emotion mapping, guided by live biometrics
  • Breathwork and grounding tied to your nervous system state
  • Tracks your window of tolerance over time
For leadership decisions

Discern

Check in with your body before a significant decision. Are you expanded or contracted? Grounded or reactive? Know whether you're deciding from clarity or from stress — and if not, get centred first.

  • Somatic check-in before high-stakes decisions
  • Decision journal that captures body signal alongside the outcome
  • Pattern recognition: how your body signals have predicted outcomes over time
  • Integrates with Oura Ring, Whoop, and Apple Watch
How it works

Not another
mindfulness timer.

We use your wearable's data — HRV, heart rate, respiratory rate — as a real-time signal, not a retrospective score. The practice happens in the moment when it counts.

01

Your body sends a signal

Your wearable notices a shift — HRV drop, elevated heart rate, change in respiration. Or you notice something yourself: a tightness, a heaviness, a decision looming.

02

Name what's present

What's in the way, or what's here to be felt? Where do you feel it in your body? The app translates sensation into language — without requiring you to have it figured out first.

03

Work with it, not against it

Regulation or inquiry — depending on what you need. Breathwork calibrated to your current state. Reflection prompts grounded in what your body reported.

04

Build the pattern over time

Sessions accumulate into a picture of your nervous system — what triggers you, what resources you, and whether your body signals have been a reliable guide to your decisions.
